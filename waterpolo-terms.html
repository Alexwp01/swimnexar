Water Polo Tryout — Terms & Conditions

Nexar Water Polo · Wesley Chapel, FL · Last updated: January 2026

1. Purpose of the Trial Session

The initial practice session is an introductory evaluation designed to assess the participant's safety, comfort level, and readiness for continued water polo training. Participation in the trial does not guarantee enrollment in the program.

2. Minimum Requirements

To participate safely, all athletes must be able to:

  • Swim independently in deep water
  • Tread water without assistance for a sustained period
  • Follow verbal instructions from the coach

If a participant cannot demonstrate these skills, the coach reserves the right to modify or end the session for safety reasons.

3. Required Equipment

  • Swimsuit (one-piece or swim briefs/jammers)
  • Swim goggles
  • Water bottle
  • Towel
  • Hair tied back (if applicable)

No jewelry, sharp objects, or loose clothing permitted in the pool.

4. Inherent Risks & Liability Waiver

By registering for this session, the parent or legal guardian acknowledges that water polo involves inherent physical risks, including but not limited to:

  • Slips and falls on wet surfaces
  • Water inhalation
  • Physical collisions with other participants or pool equipment
  • Fatigue from physical exertion

To the fullest extent permitted under Florida law, Swimnexar / Nexar Water Polo, its coaches, staff, and volunteers shall not be held liable for any injury, loss, or damage arising from participation in this session, except in cases of gross negligence or intentional misconduct.

5. Health & Medical Disclosure

Parents and guardians confirm that the participant has no undisclosed medical conditions, allergies, or physical limitations that would make participation unsafe. If a condition exists, it must be disclosed to the coach prior to entering the water.

6. Coach & Staff Authority

Coaches and staff may remove a participant from the session immediately and without prior warning if the participant:

  • Appears unsafe or physically unready
  • Becomes distressed or overwhelmed
  • Violates pool or safety rules
  • Behaves in a manner that endangers themselves or others

7. Parent / Guardian Presence

Parents or guardians may remain inside the facility to observe or wait outside/in their vehicle. All guardians must remain reachable by phone throughout the session and ensure prompt pick-up at the end of the session.

8. Media Consent

Swimnexar / Nexar Water Polo may photograph and video participants during sessions for use in social media, marketing, promotional materials, and internal training review. By registering, you grant Swimnexar the right to use such media without additional compensation or ongoing consent requirements. If you do not consent, please notify the coach in writing before the session begins.
