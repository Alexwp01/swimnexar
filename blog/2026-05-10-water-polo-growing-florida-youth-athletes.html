Water Polo Is Growing in Florida — And Your Young Athlete Can Be Part of It

May 10, 2026 · Nexar Aquatic Academy

“USA Water Polo, Florida High School Athletic Associations Join to Grow Sport - Swimming World” — Read original article

A Major Partnership Is Opening Doors for Florida's Young Water Polo Players

Exciting news is making waves across the aquatic sports community. USA Water Polo has officially partnered with Florida's high school athletic associations to help grow water polo at the youth and scholastic levels throughout the state. For families in Wesley Chapel, Land O' Lakes, and the greater Tampa Bay area, this development signals a tremendous opportunity for young athletes who are looking for a competitive, team-oriented aquatic sport.

Water polo has long been recognized as one of the most physically demanding and strategically rich sports in the aquatic world, but it has historically had a smaller footprint in Florida compared to swimming. That is now beginning to change in a meaningful way.

What This Partnership Means for Young Athletes

When organizations like USA Water Polo and state high school athletic associations align their efforts, the results can be transformative. This kind of collaboration typically leads to more sanctioned high school programs, better-trained coaches, increased access to competition, and greater visibility for athletes who are hoping to compete at the collegiate level.

For young athletes in the Tampa Bay region, this means that the path from club water polo to a high school varsity roster — and potentially beyond — is becoming more clearly defined. More schools offering the sport means more players, more competitions, and ultimately a stronger culture around water polo in our communities.

Skills That Transfer Both Ways

One of the most exciting aspects of water polo's growth is how naturally it complements swimming. Many young swimmers who are looking for a new challenge find that water polo sharpens their in-water athleticism in ways that traditional swim training does not. The sport demands explosive starts, powerful turns, vertical kicking endurance, and sharp spatial awareness — all skills that make athletes stronger in the pool across disciplines.

Conversely, swimmers who transition into water polo bring a significant advantage. Their comfort in the water, their stroke efficiency, and their cardiovascular base give them a strong foundation on which to build polo-specific skills like ball handling, positioning, and game-reading.

How Families Can Get Ahead of the Curve

If your young athlete has been curious about water polo, now is the ideal time to explore it. As the sport expands at the high school level in Florida, players who begin developing their skills early through club programs will be best positioned to make rosters and earn playing time when opportunities arise.

At Nexar Aquatic Academy, we understand the importance of building well-rounded aquatic athletes. Whether young swimmers are refining their competitive strokes or beginning to explore water polo fundamentals, strong technical training lays the groundwork for success in any aquatic pursuit. Our programs in Wesley Chapel and Land O' Lakes are designed to develop athletes who are ready to compete and grow alongside the sport.

What to Look for in a Youth Water Polo Program

  • Qualified coaching with experience in both swimming and water polo fundamentals
  • Age-appropriate skill development that emphasizes technique before competition
  • A positive team environment that keeps young athletes engaged and motivated
  • Connection to club and scholastic pathways so athletes know where the sport can take them

The Momentum Is Here — Don't Wait

The partnership between USA Water Polo and Florida's high school athletic associations is more than just a headline. It is a signal that the sport is investing in this state's future, and that future includes the Tampa Bay area. Young athletes who get involved now will be building skills, friendships, and competitive experience at exactly the right moment.
